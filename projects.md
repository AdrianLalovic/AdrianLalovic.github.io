## 1 Arduino Robotics & Autonomous Systems Project (May 2025 - August 2025) 
Personal Summer Project   

### Objective
Developed an autonomous robotic platform to gain hands‑on experience in embedded systems engineering, C++ programming, sensing technologies, and electromechanical integration. The project progressed from a basic line‑following robot to an enhanced autonomous system capable of obstacle detection, navigation, and debris collection.

### Engineering Tools and Methods  
- **Programming & Embedded Systems:** Embedded C++, Arduino framework, microcontroller programming, real‑time control logic  
- **Robotics & Automation:** Autonomous navigation, line‑following control, obstacle detection and avoidance  
- **Sensors & Hardware:** Optical and infrared sensors, DC motors and motor drivers, hardware‑software integration  
- **Electrical & Mechanical Engineering:** Electrical wiring, signal troubleshooting, mechanical assembly, rapid prototyping  
- **Engineering Practices:** Sensor calibration, system integration and testing, root‑cause analysis, iterative design improvement  

### Initial Line Following Robot  
- Built an Arduino‑based mobile robot from a commercial kit, assembling mechanical, electrical, and embedded software subsystems.
- Developed sensor‑based control algorithms in C++ to enable accurate line‑following behavior using optical sensors.
- Integrated and validated:
  - Optical / line‑tracking sensors
  - DC motors and motor driver circuits
  - Microcontroller‑based control and power architecture

### Autonomous Vacuum System Modification  
- Extended functionality beyond the original kit design to create a fully autonomous mobile robot.
- Designed and implemented additional hardware including:
  - Infrared proximity sensors for real‑time obstacle detection and avoidance
  - A custom vacuum mechanism using a repurposed computer cooling fan, mechanically and electrically integrated into the robot chassis
- Programmed C++‑based autonomous navigation logic, enabling the robot to:
  - Detect obstacles dynamically
  - Execute collision‑avoidance maneuvers
  - Navigate enclosed environments without human input

### Results
- Developed a fully autonomous robotic platform progressing from a basic line‑following system to a mobile robot capable of obstacle avoidance and debris collection.
- Achieved reliable sensor‑based control, maintaining stable line tracking under varying lighting conditions and enabling collision‑free navigation in enclosed environments using infrared sensing.
- Implemented autonomous navigation logic to detect obstacles in real time and dynamically adjust robot path without human input.
- Designed and integrated a custom vacuum mechanism alongside sensing and drive systems, ensuring continuous operation during autonomous movement.
- Improved mechanical and electrical integration to achieve robust system performance, consistent motor control, and reliable sensor feedback.
- Validated embedded control design, sensor integration, and rapid mechanical iteration through successful development of a complete electromechanical prototype.

## 2 Small Glider Design & Manufacturing Project	(September 2025 - December 2025)
Aircraft Design (AER 507): Toronto Metropolitan University  

### Objective
Design, manufacture, and test an unpowered glider optimized for minimum mass and maximum glide distance, subject to material and manufacturing constraints. Contributed to aerodynamic design, structural analysis, manufacturing, and testing

### Engineering Tools and Methods  
**Engineering Tools:**
- **CAD Software:** 3D CAD modeling of wing, fuselage, and tail geometries with emphasis on structural efficiency and manufacturability
- **MATLAB:** Aerodynamic performance estimation, glide ratio calculation, wing loading analysis, and design trade studies
- **Additive Manufacturing**: 3D printing of precision components and interfaces
- **Composites Fabrication:** Carbon fiber mold creation for strength‑to‑weight optimization
- **Manufacturing Tools:** Laser cutting, hand fabrication of wood structures, mechanical fastening, adhesive bonding

**Focus Skills & Engineering Methods:** 
- **Aerodynamic Design & Optimization:** Glide performance estimation, wing sizing, and surface finish considerations
- **Structural Analysis & Weight Reduction:** Stiffness‑to‑mass trade‑offs, load‑path awareness, and high‑stress region reinforcement
- **Design Trade Studies:** Wing area vs. weight, stiffness vs. mass, manufacturability vs. performance
- **Manufacturing & Assembly:** Subsystem integration, tolerance awareness, and material selection under constraints
- **Testing & Validation:** Flight testing, stability evaluation, performance measurement, and post‑test iteration
- **Iterative Engineering Development:** Continuous improvement beyond competition requirements using test‑driven insights

### Design & Analysis
- Used CAD to design wing, fuselage, and tail geometries with emphasis on structural efficiency
- Applied MATLAB to estimate glide ratio, wing loading, and basic aerodynamic performance
- Conducted iterative trade studies between: 
  - Wing area vs. weight
  - Structural stiffness vs. mass
  - Manufacturing complexity vs. performance

### Manufacturing & Materials
- Fabricated components using: 
  - Laser cut / hand cut wood structures
  - 3D printed components for precise interfaces
  - Carbon fiber molds for strength to weight optimization
- Integrated all subsystems using adhesive bonding and mechanical fasteners

### Results
- Delivered a fully functional, lightweight unpowered glider by combining CAD‑based design and MATLAB aerodynamic analysis to balance performance, structural efficiency, and manufacturability.
- Achieved a final mass of approximately 200 grams and demonstrated stable flight with a glide distance of approximately 20 meters, validating aerodynamic modeling and structural design decisions.
- Fabricated and integrated wood, 3D‑printed, and carbon‑fiber components, and identified post‑test improvements to further enhance strength‑to‑weight ratio and aerodynamic efficiency.

## 3 Aircraft Flight Modeling & Simulation (January 2025 - April 2025)
Flight Dynamics (AER 404): Toronto Metropolitan University

### Objective
Develop a physics‑based aircraft flight simulation capable of modeling real‑time three‑dimensional motion using first‑principles dynamics, capturing coupled translational and rotational behavior under control and environmental inputs.

### Engineering Tools and Methods  
**Engineering Tools & Software**  
- **MATLAB:** mathematical modeling, numerical integration, data analysis, simulation validation  
- **Simulink:** dynamic systems modeling, multi‑DOF simulation, control input implementation  
- **Numerical Methods:** time‑domain integration, stability verification, step‑response testing  
- **Visualization & Animation:** real‑time attitude, trajectory, and motion visualization  

**Focus Skills & Engineering Competencies**  
- **Flight Dynamics & Flight Mechanics:** physics‑based modeling of full 3‑dimensional aircraft motion
- **Equations of Motion (EOM):** translational and rotational dynamics, 9‑DOF vectored flight modeling
- **Forces & Moments Modeling:** aerodynamic forces, gravitational effects, body and inertial reference frames
- **Control Systems Modeling:** control input coupling, response analysis, scenario testing
- **Physics‑Based Simulation:** first‑principles mathematical modeling and validation prior to visualization
- **System Verification & Validation:** numerical stability checks, physical realism assessment, response testing
- **Multibody Dynamics:** coupled rotational and translational motion modeling
- **Systems Engineering:** end‑to‑end simulation workflow from theory to validated implementation
- **Technical Collaboration:** teamwork on complex system development and model integration

### Technical Contributions
- Collaborated in a 5‑person engineering team to architect and implement a complete aircraft dynamics simulation model following systems‑engineering practices.  
- Developed a first‑principles mathematical model as the simulation foundation, validating analytical correctness prior to visualization and animation.  
- Implemented full equations of motion (EOM) representing nine components of vectored flight, including:  
  - Translational dynamics in three axes  
  - Rotational dynamics using angular rates and moments  
  - Force and moment computations in both body‑fixed and inertial reference frames  
- Modeled key physical effects including:  
  - Aerodynamic lift, drag, and moments  
  - Gravitational acceleration  
   - Control surface and control‑input responses  
- Ensured correct coupling between translational and rotational dynamics to reflect realistic aircraft behavior.  

### Simulation & Visualization
- Implemented the complete dynamic model using MATLAB for numerical calculations and Simulink for modular, time‑domain system simulation.  
- Integrated a real‑time animation and visualization module to display aircraft attitude, trajectory, and motion response.  
- Verified numerical stability and physical realism through:  
  - Step‑input response testing  
  - Scenario‑based simulations  
  - Evaluation of transient and steady‑state behavior  

### Results & Outcomes
- Delivered a fully functional aircraft flight simulation framework demonstrating correct response to control and environmental inputs.  
- Successfully modeled full 3D aircraft motion, capturing coupled translational and rotational dynamics with stable numerical behavior.  
- Demonstrated physically realistic control response and motion trajectories across multiple simulation scenarios.  
- Strengthened technical understanding of flight mechanics, control coupling, rigid‑body dynamics, and model‑based simulation workflows used in aerospace engineering.

## 4 Walking Robot Design Project	(January 2025 - April 2025)
Mechanisms and Vibrations (AER 403): Toronto Metropolitan University

### Objective
Design, analyze, and fabricate a mechanically efficient walking robot using linkage‑based locomotion, with emphasis on kinematic feasibility, dynamic stability, vibration behavior, and manufacturability. The objective was to develop a single‑motor, single‑degree‑of‑freedom walking mechanism capable of traversing a three‑meter course under competition constraints.

### Engineering Tools and Methods  
**Engineering Tools**  
- **MATLAB:** kinematic modeling, displacement and velocity analysis, motion simulation, trade‑study evaluation  
- **SolidWorks:** 3D CAD modeling, mechanism assembly, constraint validation, manufacturability assessment  
- **OnShape:** parametric CAD modeling, linkage layout refinement, collaborative design iteration  
- **Laser Cutting:** fabrication of lightweight wooden structural components for rapid prototyping  

**Engineering Methods**
- **Kinematic Analysis:** Evaluated position, velocity, and motion profiles of linkage‑based locomotion mechanisms.  
- **Mechanism Synthesis:** Designed and compared four‑bar, slider‑crank, and multi‑link creeping mechanisms to identify optimal locomotion strategies.  
- **Degrees‑of‑Freedom (DOF) Analysis:** Assessed and selected single‑DOF mechanisms to maximize mechanical efficiency and control simplicity.  
- **Engineering Trade Studies:** Analyzed gait stability versus system complexity, torque requirements versus motion smoothness, and performance versus manufacturability.  
- **Motion Simulation & Visualization:** Used animation and simulation to evaluate gait behavior, ground contact consistency, and overall motion feasibility.  
- **Linkage‑Based Mechanical Design:** Designed mechanically driven locomotion systems with attention to load paths and motion translation.  
- **Dynamic Stability & Vibration Considerations:** Incorporated stability and vibration effects into mechanism selection and refinement.  
- **Design for Manufacturability (DFM):** Applied DFM principles to ensure feasible fabrication and assembly.  
- **Prototype Fabrication & Assembly:** Built and assembled physical prototypes for validation.  
- **Experimental Validation & Performance Testing:** Verified simulated behavior through physical testing.  
- **Systems Engineering Methods:** Performed concept evaluation and down‑selection using simulation results, followed by iterative design refinement.  
- **Team‑Based Engineering & Verification:** Collaborated in a team environment and verified performance against defined system and competition constraints.  

### Technical Contributions  
- Led the conceptual design, analysis, and manufacturing of a walking robot (Creeper) for the AER403 Walking Robot Competition, executing the full development cycle from concept selection through prototype testing.  
- Evaluated multiple candidate locomotion mechanisms, including:  
  - Four‑bar linkage  
  - Slider‑crank mechanism  
  - Multi‑link creeping mechanism  
- Developed MATLAB‑based kinematic models for each candidate to analyze:  
  - Link displacement and velocity profiles  
  - Gait consistency and ground contact behavior  
  - Mechanical efficiency and feasibility  
- Generated motion animations and quantitative performance metrics to support design trade‑off analysis between stability, torque requirements, and system complexity.  
- Selected the Creeper mechanism based on superior kinematic smoothness, single‑degree‑of‑freedom motion, reduced torque demand, and improved ground contact stability.  
- Created detailed CAD models using SolidWorks and OnShape, validating constraints, linkage motion, and manufacturability prior to fabrication.  
- Fabricated a lightweight prototype using laser‑cut wooden components, integrating mechanical joints and drive transmission with a single motor input.  

### Results & Outcomes  
- Delivered a fully functional walking robot prototype capable of stable, repeatable locomotion across the competition course using a single motor and linkage‑based mechanism.  
- Achieved smooth and mechanically efficient conversion of rotational input into forward translational motion through a single‑DOF linkage system.  
- Demonstrated improved gait stability and motion consistency compared to alternative mechanisms evaluated during concept selection.  
- Validated kinematic predictions through experimental testing, confirming realistic motion behavior and controlled vibration response.  
- Successfully met performance, stability, and manufacturability requirements under competition constraints.  
- Strengthened applied understanding of mechanisms design, kinematics, vibration principles, and engineering trade‑off analysis consistent with real‑world mechanical product development.

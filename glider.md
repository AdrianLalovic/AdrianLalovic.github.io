## Small Glider Design & Manufacturing Project  (September 2025 - December 2025)
Aircraft Design (AER 507): Toronto Metropolitan University  

### Objective
Design, manufacture, and test an unpowered glider optimized for minimum mass and maximum glide distance, subject to material and manufacturing constraints. Contributed to aerodynamic design, structural analysis, manufacturing, and testing

### Engineering Tools and Methods  
**Engineering Tools:**
- **CAD Software:** 3D CAD modeling of wing, fuselage, and tail geometries with emphasis on structural efficiency and manufacturability
- **MATLAB:** Aerodynamic performance estimation, glide ratio calculation, wing loading analysis, and design trade studies
- **Additive Manufacturing**: 3D printing of precision components and interfaces
- **Composites Fabrication:** Carbon fiber mold creation for strength‑to‑weight optimization
- **Manufacturing Tools:** Laser cutting, hand fabrication of wood structures, mechanical fastening, adhesive bonding

**Focus Skills & Engineering Methods:** 
- **Aerodynamic Design & Optimization:** Glide performance estimation, wing sizing, and surface finish considerations
- **Structural Analysis & Weight Reduction:** Stiffness‑to‑mass trade‑offs, load‑path awareness, and high‑stress region reinforcement
- **Design Trade Studies:** Wing area vs. weight, stiffness vs. mass, manufacturability vs. performance
- **Manufacturing & Assembly:** Subsystem integration, tolerance awareness, and material selection under constraints
- **Testing & Validation:** Flight testing, stability evaluation, performance measurement, and post‑test iteration
- **Iterative Engineering Development:** Continuous improvement beyond competition requirements using test‑driven insights

### Design & Analysis
- Used CAD to design wing, fuselage, and tail geometries with emphasis on structural efficiency
- Applied MATLAB to estimate glide ratio, wing loading, and basic aerodynamic performance
- Conducted iterative trade studies between: 
  - Wing area vs. weight
  - Structural stiffness vs. mass
  - Manufacturing complexity vs. performance

### Manufacturing & Materials
- Fabricated components using: 
  - Laser cut / hand cut wood structures
  - 3D printed components for precise interfaces
  - Carbon fiber molds for strength to weight optimization
- Integrated all subsystems using adhesive bonding and mechanical fasteners

### Results
- Delivered a fully functional, lightweight unpowered glider by combining CAD‑based design and MATLAB aerodynamic analysis to balance performance, structural efficiency, and manufacturability.
- Achieved a final mass of approximately 200 grams and demonstrated stable flight with a glide distance of approximately 20 meters, validating aerodynamic modeling and structural design decisions.
- Fabricated and integrated wood, 3D‑printed, and carbon‑fiber components, and identified post‑test improvements to further enhance strength‑to‑weight ratio and aerodynamic efficiency.
